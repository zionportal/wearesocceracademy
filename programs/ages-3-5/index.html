Ages 3-5 Soccer Program

A playful first soccer experience for young children who are learning how to move, listen, share space, and enjoy the ball.

Who it is for

For first-time and early beginner players

This program is built for preschool and kindergarten-age players who need simple instructions, short activities, encouragement, and plenty of movement. No soccer experience is expected.

Development goals

  • Build comfort with the ball at the feet
  • Improve balance, coordination, and body awareness
  • Practice listening, waiting, and following simple directions
  • Grow confidence in a group setting

Skills taught

Simple soccer foundations

  • Dribbling in open space
  • Stopping and changing direction
  • Light shooting activities
  • Games that teach teamwork and sharing

Session experience

Fun, short, and active

Families should expect upbeat activities, coach-led games, water breaks as needed, and a pace that respects young attention spans. The focus is not pressure; it is helping kids feel safe, included, and excited to return.

Confidence and teamwork

Players learn to try, celebrate small wins, take turns, listen to coaches, and interact positively with other children.

Beginner guidance

What families should expect

  • Some players may need time to separate from parents or join the group.
  • Comfort and participation usually grow with repetition.
  • Families can choose Not Sure Yet on the form if they want help selecting a program.

FAQ

Does my child need prior soccer experience?

No. This age group is designed for first exposure and early confidence.

What if my child is shy?

That is normal at this age. The goal is to help players warm up at a comfortable pace.

Is this competitive?

No. The emphasis is movement, ball comfort, listening, and fun.
