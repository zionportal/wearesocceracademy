Questions

Frequently asked questions

Which program should my child join?

Start with the age ranges shown above. If your child is newer or more advanced than the typical range, request information and ask for placement guidance.

What should players bring?

Players should bring water, comfortable athletic clothing, and safe soccer or athletic shoes. Specific equipment needs can be confirmed before the first session.

Do you offer programs for schools or groups?

Yes. The academy can support school, rec center, and community programming with flexible formats based on age group, schedule, and available space.

Does my child need soccer experience?

No. Beginners are welcome. Coaches help players learn at a comfortable pace while building confidence, coordination, and comfort with the ball.

How are players grouped?

Players are generally grouped by age and readiness. If a player needs a different level of challenge, we can help recommend the best fit.

What does a typical session include?

Sessions usually include warmups, ball touches, skill games, small-sided play, teamwork moments, and simple coaching feedback.

Do you focus on fun or competition?

Both matter, but fun comes first for young players. We use positive competition and game-based learning to help kids improve without unnecessary pressure.

Can parents watch training?

Yes, parents are welcome to watch unless a specific location has its own viewing rules. We want families to feel comfortable and informed.

What happens if weather affects a session?

If weather or field conditions make training unsafe, families will be updated as early as possible with cancellation, delay, or makeup information.

How do we register or request information?

Use the registration form below the FAQ section. Share your parent information, player details, location preference, and program interest.
